Palace photos

The rose-colored facades of the Pink City's grand forts and palaces make terrific photo backdrops. You can snap a selfie framed by over 900 windows at the iconic Hawa Mahal, or roam around the City Palace’s checkered floors, marble pillars, and arched gateways.

Jaipur stepwells

Jaipur’s centuries-old stepwells were designed not only to store rainwater but also, with their zigzagging stairways and intricate arches, to look majestic. Few sites allow visitors to descend the stairs, but you can admire and photograph the strikingly geometrical constructions.

Bazaar shopping

Bargain for traditional cloth puppets, handmade leather jootis & fine pottery at vibrant bazaars.

Old City rickshaw tour

A rickshaw is the perfect vehicle to explore the narrow alleys of Jaipur's Old City, and tour companies offer pedal-powered or electric rides.

About&History

Jaipur is the capital and the largest city of the Indian state of Rajasthan.The city of Jaipur was founded by King of Amer, Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II on 18 November 1727, who ruled from 1699 to 1743. He planned to shift his capital from Amer, 11 kilometres to Jaipur to accommodate the growing population and increasing scarcity of water. Jai Singh consulted several books on architecture and architects while planning the layout of Jaipur. Under the architectural guidance of Vidyadhar Bhattacharya, Jaipur was planned based on the principles of Vastu Shastra and Shilpa Shastra.
